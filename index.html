Rocketry Projects

My highschool offered a three year rocketry program under the System's Go curriculum in which students are tasked with researching, designing, and constructing three different high-powered rockets each with its own goal. Throughout the program in addition to the design process, we built mathmatical and CAD models in Excel, Google Sheets, Fusion 360, and Solidworks. These models were then presented to NASA, Boeing, and other experts to ensure the success of our designs. Below I have detailed the different projects and my roles. Tsiolkovsky Project

The Tsiolkovsky or 1 pound/1 mile level of the Systems Go curriculum, students are tasked with bringing a one pound payload to an altitude of one mile with a given budget of 1,000 dollars. Painted in red and black it reached a altitude of roughly 4000 feet and included an on board camera which recorded the entire flight.

Oberth Project
The Oberth or Transonic level of the Systems Go curriculum, students are tasked with breaking the sounds barrier while remaining under 13,000 feet with a given budget of 900 dollars. A team picture, taken before the flight, is shown above. While not confirmed due to circumstances outside our control the results of the flight are currently a final alitude of greater then 10,000 feet and top speed of roughly mach 1.07.
Goddard Project
The Goddard and final year of the program, students are tasked with designing a rocket that can carry a 5lb payload to world-record breaking altitude of 50,000 feet with a given budget of 10,000 dollars. Shown above is a picture taken right on launch with the results fo the flight being a world record breaking alitude of 49,523 feet ASL and top speed of mach 1.6.
